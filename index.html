Web Writing Sample 1: Diogenes of Sinope

I was tasked with creating and styling a web page for a historical or fictional character, and I couldn't shake the idea that I should create something for a historical figure that would have absolutely hated the prospect of a personalized website. I chose to create this for Diogenes of Sinope (whose Wikipedia page is linked in the sample), the ancient Greek who is largely credited with originating philosohpical cynicism.

I've had this 19th century painting by John William Waterhouse of him saved among my "inspiration" pieces for some time now, and I wanted to use it as my color palette for the page. Specifically, the page background is picked from the staircase (thematically, the setting), his job title from the sky (ironically, he prioritized humility), the text from the shadows (pragmatically, for high contrast), and the links from one of the women's toga (symbolically, he wants them to go away and links take you away).

The font style was taken from perusing websafe, handwritten fonts, and is "Permanent Marker".

Web Writing Sample 2: Moe's Tavern

For this project, two of my fellow graduate students, Vicky Banuelos and Nafisa Mohammed, and I worked on creating a busienss website for a fictional business: Moe's Tavern from the probably-too-long-running show The Simpsons. We collaborated on the design and content of the site, but my focus was on creating an early-aughts style, multimedia "Easter Egg" for the
Not Allowed! page, to incorporate a bit of the source material and pay homage to the Internet exploration of my youth.

As explained in our style and content guides, inaccuracies, typos, and graphic design hideousness are intended to be diegetic. My fork of the repository can be found here.
